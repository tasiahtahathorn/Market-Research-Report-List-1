<p><ul><li>Mechanical Lidar</li><li>Solid State Lidar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive LiDAR system market includes two primary types: mechanical LiDAR and solid-state LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR employs rotating components to capture a 360-degree view, offering high accuracy and range but being more complex and prone to wear. In contrast, solid-state LiDAR utilizes a fixed design with no moving parts, leading to enhanced durability, compactness, and typically lower manufacturing costs. Each type serves distinct applications within autonomous vehicles, impacting performance, reliability, and integration strategies in the automotive sector.</p></p>
