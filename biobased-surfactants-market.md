<p><p>The biobased surfactants market is categorized into four main types: anionic, cationic, nonionic, and amphoteric surfactants. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge and are effective in cleansing and emulsifying. Cationic surfactants possess a positive charge and are often used in fabric softeners and disinfectants. Nonionic surfactants have no charge, providing versatility in various applications like detergents. Amphoteric surfactants can carry both positive and negative charges, making them suitable for a wide range of formulations, including personal care products.</p></p>
